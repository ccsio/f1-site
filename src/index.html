Who are we?


We are Team Icarus, a Luxembourgish F1 in Schools team established in October 2022. We strive to constantntly improve our car design, communication and presentation. Through our participation in several events, we have managed to craft extremely effective, as well as visually-appealing car winning 2 awards.


Our first event


Our first race was in December of 2022: the Regional Finals in Luxembourg. This is where we managed to win the Judges‘ Favourite award, only 2 months after our entry to the project. Considering the time limit, our inexperience, all the challenges of this competition, and our lack of resources, this was an immense and unprecedented success, source of our motivation and ambition to strive for excellence today.

June 2023


Whilst preparing for our next competition, we recruited 3 new members, who have immeasurably boosted the teams‘ moral, productivity and spirit. At our first event with out new team, in June of 2023, we were awarded the ‘Regional Top Team’, having outperformed the competition since preparation.


Showrun September 2023


Most recently, in September of 2023, we participated in ISL’s F1 in Schools ‘Showrun’, where we got to present our pit display, team and our car. This event provided invaluable experience to our team, strengthening bonds and communications between members, as well as with other teams.


Our current project is the qualification to the 2024 World Finals, in hope of representing Luxembourg on the international scale.

F1 in Schools


F1 in Schools is an international educational program that encourages students to learn about science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) subjects through designing, building, and racing miniature Formula One cars. The program promotes hands-on learning and helps students develop important skills such as teamwork, problem-solving, and project management. By participating in F1 in Schools, students have the opportunity to showcase their creativity, work with cutting-edge technology, and potentially pursue careers in STEM fields. This program is open to students of all ages and is a fun and engaging way to learn about STEM subjects and develop valuable life skills.
